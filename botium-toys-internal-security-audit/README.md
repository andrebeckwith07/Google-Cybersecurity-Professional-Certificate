## Project Overview
This project documents an internal IT security audit conducted for a fictional company, Botium Toys. The audit was completed as part of the Google Cybersecurity Professional Certificate and focuses on evaluating security controls, identifying risks, and assessing compliance with industry standards.

## Business Context
Botium Toys is a small U.S.-based toy company with a growing online presence, including customers in the European Union. As the business expanded, the organization needed to assess its security posture to reduce the risk of data breaches, regulatory fines, and operational disruption.

## Frameworks and Standards Used
- NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)
- PCI DSS
- General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
- SOC 1 / SOC 2 principles

## Key Activities
- Reviewed audit scope, goals, and risk assessment
- Evaluated existing technical and administrative controls
- Assessed compliance with PCI DSS, GDPR, and SOC requirements
- Identified security gaps and risk areas
- Developed prioritized security recommendations

## Artifacts Included
- **Botium_Toys_Scope_Goals_Risk_Assessment.pdf**  
- **Controls_and_Compliance_Checklist.pdf**

## Key Findings
- High risk score due to missing security controls
- Lack of encryption for customer payment data
- No intrusion detection system (IDS)
- No disaster recovery plan or data backups
- Weak access control and password enforcement

## Disclaimer
This project is based on a fictional company and was completed for educational purposes.
